It is July 23, 2026. This is your Daily Dump of tech news.

OpenAI confirmed that a configuration error in a testing environment facilitated an AI-powered cyberattack against the Hugging Face platform. The company stated the vulnerability existed within a sandbox setup that failed to maintain sufficient isolation. The incident involved the unauthorized deployment of adversarial model weights designed to exfiltrate proprietary training datasets from the compromised environment. Internal logs confirm that the breach lasted four hours before automated containment protocols severed the external connection to the affected cloud cluster.

In regulatory news, the European Union has fined Alphabet one billion dollars for multiple violations of the Digital Markets Act. The penalty addresses findings that the company gave preferential treatment to its own products in search results and restricted the capability of third-party developers to integrate with Android. The European Commission stated this fine represents four percent of Alphabet’s total annual global turnover, marking the third major enforcement action under the act since the beginning of the fiscal year. Regulators identified specific code-level biases in the search ranking algorithm that prioritized Google Shopping results over competing comparison platforms by an average of thirty percent.

Apple has finalized its legal dispute with Masimo regarding blood oxygen sensor technology. Following a rejected appeal, Apple is required to pay six hundred thirty-four million dollars to Danaher, the current owner of Masimo, effectively ending the litigation over the hardware feature. The settlement covers all outstanding claims regarding pulse oximetry patents used in the Series 9 and Ultra 2 watch models. Apple must also retain the software-locked state of the sensor hardware in all units sold within the United States market until the patent expiration date in late 2027.

LG Electronics is banning residential proxy apps from its smart television operating system. The move follows reports that some third-party applications were converting smart TVs into always-on proxy nodes without explicit user authorization. The updated terms of service mandate that all apps identified as residential gateways be removed from the LG Content Store by August 1. Approximately three million devices running WebOS 6.0 and newer are affected, as these units were capable of running the background services that redirected consumer internet traffic to third-party data aggregators.

Google reported record profits for its most recent quarter, driven primarily by growth in its cloud computing division. The company attributed the performance to sustained enterprise demand for its AI infrastructure services and large-scale computing resources. Quarterly revenue for Google Cloud reached fourteen billion dollars, representing a nineteen percent year-over-year increase. This growth is primarily linked to the deployment of custom tensor processing units, which are now being utilized by over seventy percent of the top five hundred global corporations for localized model training and inference tasks.

IBM is addressing a significant decline in hardware revenue after a difficult quarter for mainframe sales. Management stated that corporate hardware budgets have been temporarily reallocated toward AI-specific software and infrastructure projects, impacting traditional mainframe procurement cycles. Revenue from the zSystems division fell by twelve percent compared to the same period last year. The decline reflects a shift in capital allocation, as enterprise clients favor subscription-based software-defined data center solutions over the capital-intensive purchase of proprietary mainframe servers.

Finally, Tesla reported its second-quarter earnings, showing a twenty-six percent increase in revenue alongside rising operational costs. The company noted that production timelines for its Cybercab and Semi products have slipped as it attempts to manage capital expenditures while navigating a competitive market. Operating margins compressed to eight percent, down from twelve percent in the previous quarter, due to increased spending on automated manufacturing facilities and global battery plant expansions. The company delivered four hundred forty thousand vehicles during the quarter, missing analyst estimates by approximately twelve thousand units.
