It is July 19, 2026. This is your Daily Dump of tech news.

WordPress has issued an emergency patch for critical remote code execution flaws in its core software, identified as the wp2shell vulnerability. This vulnerability affects all WordPress installations running versions 6.4 through 6.5.2, creating a direct path for unauthenticated attackers to gain full administrative access to database systems. Public exploits for the flaw are currently circulating on multiple dark web forums, allowing unauthorized users to execute arbitrary code on affected websites. The patch modifies the core input validation routines that previously failed to sanitize serialized data, a process that previously allowed for complete server takeover within milliseconds of an automated request.

On the federal policy front, the Department of Justice has reversed its ban on TikTok for government-issued devices. This change follows a three-year federal directive that prohibited the application on approximately four million government-managed mobile endpoints. Federal employees are now permitted to download and use the application on their work phones again, marking a significant shift in federal mobile device management and security compliance. The reversal stems from a new data-sharing agreement between the agency and the platform’s parent company, which mandates that all user metadata associated with government accounts must be stored exclusively on domestic servers maintained by a third-party audit firm.

Google is shifting its emoji strategy by open-sourcing its entire library of three-dimensional emoji assets. The library contains over three thousand distinct pictograms, all rendered in the Noto Color Emoji typeface. Developers and designers can now integrate these assets into their own software and creative projects, as the company releases the technical documentation and design specifications that guided the original creation of the pictograms. This release replaces the previous proprietary licensing model that restricted the use of these specific 3D assets to Google’s own mobile and web platforms, moving the assets to the Apache License 2.0.

In autonomous transit, Waymo suspended its San Francisco operations for one hour yesterday due to a localized power outage. The service pause impacted the company’s fleet availability across the city, affecting all five hundred active vehicles operating in the region. Operations resumed once power was restored to the impacted infrastructure, which includes the grid nodes necessary to maintain real-time telemetry and remote assistance connections. This is the second instance of a total fleet stoppage in San Francisco this year, with the previous event lasting forty-five minutes during a regional network synchronization error in March.

Moving to healthcare regulation, the federal government is moving forward with pilot programs to integrate artificial intelligence into insurance prior authorization workflows. The program is launching across twelve major insurance providers, covering approximately forty million policyholders. The goal is to automate coverage decisions for patients, replacing manual review processes that historically took between three and five business days with algorithmic assessment of medical necessity. These systems utilize historical claims data and clinical guidelines to evaluate procedure requests in under sixty seconds, shifting the reliance from human claims adjusters to pattern-recognition models.

Finally, game developer ZA/UM has announced a workforce reduction affecting thirty-two employees. This reduction represents twelve percent of the total company headcount. The layoffs occur two months after the studio's release of its most recent title, Zero Parades for Dead Spies, which recorded initial sales of four hundred thousand units. The affected roles span across the engineering, narrative design, and art departments, following a strategic pivot by the studio to consolidate its development pipeline into a single, unified project focus for the upcoming fiscal year.
