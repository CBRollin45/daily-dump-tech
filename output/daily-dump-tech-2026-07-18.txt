Apple has increased subscription prices for Apple Music, marking the first rate adjustment since twenty-twenty-two. Individual plans now cost eleven dollars and ninety-nine cents per month, while family plans have risen to nineteen dollars and ninety-nine cents. The company cited rising music licensing fees as the reason for the change. These new rates represent a two-dollar increase for individual subscribers and a three-dollar increase for family accounts, which support up to six users. The previous pricing structure had remained static for four years, during which time industry data shows global streaming royalty payouts to labels and publishers have increased by an average of six percent annually. This adjustment aligns Apple Music pricing with competitors like Spotify and YouTube Music, which implemented similar hikes across their premium tiers during the last eighteen months.

In AI software, Google is rebranding its research tool NotebookLM to Gemini Notebook. The company is also integrating these notebook capabilities directly into its AI-powered search interface. This transition moves the tool from a standalone experimental product under the Labs division into the core Gemini ecosystem. Users can now access source-grounding features, which allow the AI to synthesize information from uploaded documents and PDFs, directly within the primary search results page. The integration replaces the former link-out workflow, allowing the model to process up to two million tokens of context simultaneously. This change moves the product away from its original prototype phase, which launched in twenty-twenty-three, and into the standard feature set for all enterprise and consumer Gemini accounts.

On the social media front, TikTok is launching an opt-in tool designed to scan for and detect AI-generated likenesses. The feature allows creators to report unauthorized use of their digital likeness, with initial testing currently limited to select creators in the United States. The system utilizes digital watermarking and biometric scanning to compare video uploads against a creator’s verified database of prior content. According to company disclosures, the tool identifies synthetic media that deviates from a user’s historical facial and vocal patterns by more than fifteen percent. This initiative follows federal legislation passed earlier this year requiring platforms to provide mechanisms for the rapid removal of non-consensual deepfake content. Creators who report a match receive an automated notification for content takedown within twenty-four hours of the initial scan.

Turning to legal developments, Apple has filed a lawsuit against OpenAI. The public complaint alleges various undisclosed violations by the AI firm, though the specific nature of the claims has prompted debate regarding standard industry practices versus actionable legal grievances. The filing centers on the unauthorized ingestion of proprietary data sets used during the training of large language models. Apple contends that these data sets included internal software documentation and restricted hardware specifications obtained without a licensing agreement. The complaint seeks an injunction against the further use of this training data and requests the deletion of all model weights derived from the contested information. This action marks the first time a major hardware manufacturer has sought a court order to force the destruction of a specific model iteration based on intellectual property claims related to training data sourcing.

In the enterprise sector, Databricks has reached a market valuation of one hundred eighty-eight billion dollars. The company has shifted its corporate focus toward AI services and recently published research highlighting cost efficiencies for using open-weight models in software development. This valuation reflects a thirty-two percent increase since their last funding round in the fourth quarter of twenty-twenty-five. The shift in focus involves prioritizing their MosaicML acquisition, which facilitates the deployment of open-source models on private infrastructure. Recent internal benchmarks published by the firm indicate that organizations can reduce inference costs by forty percent by utilizing optimized open-weight models compared to proprietary application programming interfaces. The company now services over ten thousand enterprise customers, with data processing volumes growing by twenty-five percent quarter-over-quarter.

Regarding environmental monitoring, Google-backed satellites have launched as part of the FireSat program. These satellites are designed to identify wildfire ignition points with greater accuracy and speed than existing orbital infrastructure. The constellation utilizes medium-wave infrared sensors capable of detecting heat signatures as small as five meters in diameter. This resolution is ten times higher than that of existing weather satellites currently utilized by national forestry services. By processing thermal data onboard the satellite, the system transmits coordinates to ground stations within twenty minutes of a fire’s start, significantly faster than the current two-hour processing window for manual satellite imagery review. The program aims to cover ninety-five percent of global forest land by the end of twenty-twenty-eight.

Finally, OnePlus has officially ceased operations in the United States and Canada. The smartphone manufacturer announced the closure through official community forums, citing a strategic change in corporate direction. This exit includes the immediate termination of direct-to-consumer sales, the closure of all regional service centers, and the cessation of technical support for devices sold in these markets after July thirty-first. The company previously held a three percent market share in the United States smartphone segment, primarily driven by mid-range device sales. All existing warranties for devices purchased in these regions will be honored through third-party logistics providers for the remainder of their coverage period. The company will now focus its global sales operations exclusively on the European, Indian, and Chinese markets.
