Shot-scraper version one point ten is out, introducing "shot-scraper video." This tool accepts a storyboard dot YML file, defining a sequence of interactions, navigations, and assertions for a web application. It uses Playwright to control a headless browser and record a video of that routine. Shot-scraper previously captured static screenshots; this new feature allows developers to capture dynamic interactions and user flows. For developers working with coding agents, this is a significant addition. It provides an automatable way for agents to record and demonstrate their work. Instead of a final screenshot, an agent can now present a video showing the exact steps it took, invaluable for debugging or presenting capabilities.

Moving on, Git version two point five five has enabled Rust support by default. This is part of the Git project's ongoing strategy to migrate core components from C to Rust, a move discussed over several releases. The primary motivation is Rust's memory safety guarantees. C is prone to memory-related bugs leading to vulnerabilities and crashes. Rust's ownership and borrowing system eliminates these errors at compile time, leading to more robust code. While the entire codebase isn't being rewritten, enabling Rust support signifies deeper integration. This update also fixes how interactive rebase handles merge commits. Previously, complex rebase operations could corrupt merge histories. This fix improves the reliability of history rewriting, making it safer.

Elsewhere, Meta's new Fury AI Glasses are garnering positive reviews for smart features like real-time translation, object identification, and Conversation Focus, which transcribes and summarizes spoken conversations. However, Meta quietly announced "rate limits" on Conversation Focus, capping it at three hours per month. This limitation wasn't prominent in initial marketing. To get unlimited access, users reportedly need a nineteen dollar and ninety-nine cent per month Meta One Premium subscription. This introduces a soft paywall for a core AI feature, transforming a hardware purchase into a recurring subscription for full functionality.

In AI, Google's NotebookLM is adding a new feature that summarizes research into TikTok-style AI-generated video clips. NotebookLM is Google's AI-powered research assistant. This new capability is rolling out to Google AI Ultra and Pro subscribers. The system will analyze uploaded materials, like research papers, and generate concise, sixty-second vertical video summaries. These videos mimic short-form, rapid-consumption styles, providing a quick, visually digestible way to grasp main points or catch up on notes efficiently.

Turning to security, Amazon has been fined two point two five million dollars by the Federal Trade Commission. This fine settles claims that Amazon failed to adequately assist customers who fell victim to identity theft. The FTC's complaint accuses Amazon of a systemic failure to provide identity theft victims with crucial information about purchases made with their stolen credentials. Amazon allegedly refused to disclose details like items purchased, shipping addresses, or IP addresses used by fraudsters. This lack of transparency hindered victims' ability to report fraud accurately to law enforcement and financial institutions, delaying recovery. The FTC emphasized companies' responsibility to cooperate with victims.

Over at Google, Vinton Cerf, co-creator of TCP/IP protocols and often called one of the "Fathers of the Internet," is retiring as Google's Chief Internet Evangelist next week. Cerf's career spans the internet's modern history. He was instrumental in designing the fundamental communication protocols underpinning the global network. Beyond technical contributions, Cerf was a prominent advocate for the internet's open principles and continued development. In his Google role since 2005, he championed internet accessibility and digital preservation. His retirement marks the end of an era for one of digital communication's most influential pioneers.

Finally, Reddit is implementing a new policy requiring users to log in to access old dot Reddit dot com. The old dot Reddit dot com interface provides a simpler, less resource-intensive view, preferred by many long-time users. The company states this change is due to logged-out access being a "significant source of abusive scraping." Abusive scraping refers to unauthorized automated data extraction, which can strain servers and be used for training large language models without permission. By requiring a login, Reddit can implement stronger rate limiting and bot detection, making it harder for automated scripts to scrape content. This aims to curb unauthorized data extraction and improve security, though it will impact users who prefer browsing the classic Reddit experience without logging in.
