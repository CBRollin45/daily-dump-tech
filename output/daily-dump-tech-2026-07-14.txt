It is July 14, 2026. This is your Daily Dump of tech news.

Apple has released the first public betas for macOS Golden Gate and iOS 27. These builds are now accessible via the Apple beta software portal for all enrolled participants. The release integrates the revamped Siri artificial intelligence suite, which utilizes on-device large language models to manage multi-app workflows. This replaces the previous Siri architecture that relied predominantly on cloud-based intent parsing and limited command-and-control logic. The update also includes expanded API hooks for third-party applications, allowing developers to surface real-time data within the primary interface. Previously, these features were restricted to the developer preview environments released at the Worldwide Developers Conference last month.

Microsoft is testing a stripped-down version of the Windows 11 search menu. This experimental interface removes the recommended content section, the trending search bar, and the integrated advertising modules that currently occupy roughly thirty percent of the search panel’s vertical space. The update is live in the Canary and Dev channels for Windows Insiders, replacing the current ad-heavy layout with a minimalist list restricted to local files and system settings. This change represents a shift away from the company’s recent strategy of using the search interface as a promotional surface for Microsoft 365 services and web-based advertisements.

In AI funding, the video generation startup PixVerse has raised 439 million dollars in new capital. This Series D funding round brings the company’s total valuation to 2.1 billion dollars. The investment will be deployed to increase the compute capacity of its GPU clusters, which currently host the model’s video-to-video and text-to-video generation engines. PixVerse plans to use the capital to scale its world model parameters, moving from the current 50-billion-parameter architecture to a larger model designed for high-fidelity 4K output. The funding follows recent industry trends where generative video startups shift capital expenditure toward massive-scale GPU procurement.

Turning to policy, New York has become the first state to enact a moratorium on new hyperscale data centers. The executive order signed by Governor Kathy Hochul halts the approval of new facilities exceeding 50 megawatts of power consumption for a 12-month period. The state will conduct an environmental impact review focused on the grid strain caused by these facilities, which currently account for 14 percent of the state’s total daily electricity usage. Existing data centers are excluded from the moratorium, but any pending expansion applications for current sites are frozen until the assessment concludes.

In security news, the United States government is warning that Russian state-sponsored actors are actively targeting residential routers. The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency reports that attackers are exploiting known vulnerabilities in consumer-grade firmware to establish persistent proxy networks for malicious traffic routing. The agency identified at least 15,000 compromised devices in the United States currently participating in these botnet operations. These hijacked routers are being used to mask the origin of cyberattacks targeting government infrastructure and financial institutions by cycling traffic through residential IP addresses to evade traditional blocklists.

Finally, a coalition of 12 states has filed an antitrust lawsuit to block the proposed 45-billion-dollar merger between Paramount and Warner Brothers Discovery. The attorneys general filing the suit argue that the consolidation would combine two of the five remaining major Hollywood studios, creating a firm that controls roughly 38 percent of all scripted television production in the United States. The complaint alleges that this concentration of intellectual property would result in higher subscription fees for streaming bundles and a reduction in the total volume of original programming commissioned annually. The lawsuit seeks a permanent injunction to prevent the asset transfer between the two media conglomerates.
