Microsoft is experiencing a widespread service outage impacting Teams, SharePoint, and other core services across North America. The disruption began at approximately 4:00 AM Eastern time, resulting in a total loss of connectivity for users attempting to access Microsoft 365 cloud environments. Internal logs indicate that authentication failures are the primary driver of the outage, affecting both enterprise and government cloud tenants. Microsoft has confirmed that its engineering teams are currently rerouting traffic through secondary data centers to restore functionality, though the company has not provided an estimated time for a full restoration of services.

On the hardware side, AMD is challenging Nvidia with the release of its new Helios rack-scale AI system. The Helios unit integrates eight high-bandwidth memory modules alongside custom processing silicon, designed to replace the previous generation of air-cooled server clusters with a liquid-cooled architecture. Each rack supports up to 512 gigabytes of dedicated video memory, optimized specifically for training large language models with parameters exceeding one trillion. The hardware is designed for large-scale data centers and is scheduled to begin shipping to enterprise customers later this year, with initial orders already confirmed by major cloud providers.

In AI, Anthropic has expanded the availability of its voice mode. Previously restricted to the lightweight Haiku model, voice capabilities are now integrated into the more powerful Opus and Sonnet models. These models now feature native interoperability with Gmail, Slack, and Canva, allowing the voice interface to execute tasks such as drafting emails, summarizing chat threads, and generating visual assets within third-party applications. This expansion moves the voice interface from simple conversational tasks to active workspace automation, utilizing a new multimodal processing pipeline that reduces latency by 40 percent compared to the original release.

Security researchers have identified an active exploit for a vulnerability in the Windmill platform. The flaw, tracked as CVE-2026-29059, allows unauthenticated attackers to read arbitrary files on affected servers by bypassing the platform’s primary authentication gateway. Shodan scans confirm that approximately 170 systems are currently identified as exposed globally, primarily concentrated in industrial control environments. The vulnerability resides in the way the platform handles session tokens, permitting an attacker to elevate privileges without possessing valid credentials or multi-factor authentication.

In business news, Patreon is laying off 20 percent of its workforce. The company stated that while its core subscription business remains stable, the reduction is a response to broader shifts in the market and a move to adjust its long-term cost structure. This reduction affects approximately 150 employees across the engineering, product, and marketing departments. Patreon’s leadership indicated that the move follows two years of stagnant user growth, with the company shifting focus toward consolidating its creator tools and reducing overhead related to its international office footprint.

Google is rolling out a new identity verification feature that allows users to log in using selfie videos. The system is designed for account recovery, age verification, and the generation of AI avatars, moving away from traditional password-only reliance. Users are prompted to record a five-second video, which the system compares against previously uploaded government-issued identification or existing account imagery using biometric mapping. This feature replaces the older two-factor SMS verification process for high-risk account actions, aiming to reduce the success rate of automated credential stuffing attacks by requiring live biometric input.

Turning to automotive policy, US regulators are initiating a new safety rulemaking process specifically targeting vehicle door handles. The move follows a series of reported incidents, including three fatalities, where occupants became trapped inside electronic-door vehicles during total power failures. Current federal motor vehicle safety standards require manual overrides, but regulators are investigating whether the force required to trigger these mechanical releases exceeds the physical capabilities of an average adult during an emergency. The inquiry will focus on the design of flush-mounted electronic handles that retract into the vehicle body, which have been cited in multiple National Highway Traffic Safety Administration complaints.

Finally, European researchers are reporting continued difficulty in accessing data from major technology platforms. Despite European Union laws granting rights to scrutinize tech companies for social media harms, researchers state that companies including TikTok, X, and Meta are still unilaterally controlling which external parties are granted access to their internal datasets. Reports from the European Digital Observatory indicate that current data-sharing APIs provide only 15 percent of the information necessary to audit algorithmic amplification. Consequently, academic institutions are citing an inability to verify compliance with the Digital Services Act, as companies continue to cite intellectual property and user privacy protections to restrict raw data exports.
