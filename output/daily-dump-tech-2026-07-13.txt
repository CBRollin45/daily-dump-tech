Waze is adding Google’s Gemini AI assistant to its navigation platform. The update introduces conversational reporting, allowing users to describe road hazards, construction, or accidents in natural language rather than using the existing grid of preset interface buttons. This integration replaces the standard manual input flow with a voice-activated processing layer that interprets real-time speech to update map data for the community. The system aims to provide personalized trip planning and contextual navigation assistance by analyzing historical traffic data stored in the Google Cloud platform. Previously, Waze relied on static user-reported icons to populate its map, but the new architecture processes unstructured conversational inputs to identify specific road conditions and map them to precise geographic coordinates.

On the hardware side, Hisense has released the A10, a smartphone featuring a dual-display design. The device includes a primary six-point-eight-inch OLED screen and a secondary four-inch e-ink display that is physically detachable from the rear chassis. This model follows the previous A9 version, which featured a fixed e-ink panel integrated directly into the handset housing. The A10 utilizes a magnetic pogo-pin connector to maintain data transmission when the e-ink module is attached, allowing the device to draw power from the primary battery while consuming less than ten milliwatts during static page rendering. This shift toward modularity targets the low-power reading market, replacing the need for separate dedicated e-readers by allowing users to physically remove the display to reduce device weight by forty-two grams.

Apple’s defunct self-driving car program has left a tangible impact on the company’s current hardware stack. Development of the vehicle’s autonomous systems required high-performance, low-latency on-device AI processing to handle real-time sensor fusion from lidar and optical camera arrays. This requirement forced Apple to accelerate the design of its proprietary neural engine architecture, which was originally intended to manage vehicle pathing and obstacle avoidance. These architectural optimizations for power-efficient AI compute are now utilized across the current M-series and A-series processor lineup, enabling the current generation of silicon to run large language models locally on mobile devices without thermal throttling. The shift represents a hardware pivot, as the specialized logic once reserved for the car’s drive computer is now the primary driver for on-device generative AI features in the latest desktop and mobile hardware.

Turning to regulation, the European Union is evaluating new restrictions on social media access for minors. Proposed measures include mandatory biometric or document-based age verification, outright usage bans for users under the age of thirteen, and phased access levels for those between thirteen and eighteen. Platforms would be required to provide independent audit evidence that their recommendation algorithms do not induce addictive behaviors or psychological harm to users under the age of eighteen before those services are legally accessible. These proposals follow the Digital Services Act, extending the enforcement mandate to include specific algorithmic transparency requirements for any platform with more than forty-five million monthly active users within the European Union.

In security, a major leak of police drone footage in San Francisco has revealed the scale of local urban surveillance. Hours of video captured by the San Francisco Police Department’s fleet of Skydio X10 drones were exposed on an unsecured public-facing server, detailing how the agency monitors city streets, public parks, and protest routes from the air. The files totaled approximately four terabytes of unencrypted metadata and high-definition video, including timestamps and precise GPS coordinates for each flight path. The incident demonstrates the volume of sensitive visual data being aggregated by automated aerial surveillance platforms and the vulnerabilities inherent in storing raw footage in centralized cloud repositories without granular access controls.
